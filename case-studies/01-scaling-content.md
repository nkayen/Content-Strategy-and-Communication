## CHALLENGES AND RESTRAINTS <br>
### **Managing Change and Rapid Growth** <br>
Rapid growth through multiple (and overlapping) mergers and acquisitions created significant strategic and organizational challenges, process inconsistencies, and inefficient employee onboarding. <br>
* The **challenge** was to centralize, standardize, and scale all training and knowledge systems in line with the PMO (Project Management Office) in charge of organization strategy execution, and pivot where and when required. <br>
* The **outcome** successfully aligned releases and deployments of new processes, standards, and technology to organization wide initiatives through Agile iterations and structured feedback sessions, regardless of whether these projects were done as Agile, Waterfall, Swivelchair, etc. <br><br>

### **Harmonizing a Fragmented Workforce** <br>
Centralizing de-centralized and fragmented ways of working by adapting global processes and standards, and curating and maintaining a centralized knowledge repository in line with industry standards. <br>
* The **challenge** was to ensure the process not only accurately reflected the correct industry standard process workflow, the corresponding ServiceNow process workflow, and met compliance and governance requirements, but was also agreed to by all stakeholders and reflected the most optimal way of working regardless of which party had the political overhand in the related merger/acquisition. <br>
* The **outcome** scaled the business away from silos and into a globally homogenous way of working through adaption by outcome and efficiency, rather than habits and individual practices, directly increasing client satisfaction and ease of reporting and governance through intensive workshops, analysis and iterative release management. <br><br>

### **Designing Agile Frameworks** <br>
Developing learning and communication-frameworks that could grow with the organization and would not need to be redrafted with every change of direction. <br>
* The **challenge** was to align with the organization's key communication standards and define content and communication frameworks to ensure they could remain a constant and recognizable source of information through successive organizational and strategic changes. <br>
* The **outcome** saw content and communication frameworks aligned to organizational core strategies by priority, scale, scope, and impact, rather than by locale, team, or demographic. <br><br>

### **Creating Universal Content** <br>
Creating documentation and training for new and existing employees regardless of individual background, learning style, English language level, educational background, or professional experience. <br>
* The **challenge** was to provide training in clear and simple language without losing its professional tone with the right mix of text, visuals, and interactive elements, to not only keep all learners engaged, but also actively absorbing the content. <br>
* The **outcome** facilitated ease of on-boarding and absorption, structured knowledge progression and clear, role-based learning frameworks. Further mitigating loss of information transfer between the process documentation and learning content by owning the chain of work from inception to implementation.
